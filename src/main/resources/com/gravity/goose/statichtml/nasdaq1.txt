function _NewsTicker(newsSymbol)
{
    var qtSymbol;
    var pos;
    var query = window.location.search.substring(1);
    var params = query.split('&');
    var parm;
    var consumer = '';
    var i;

    for (i=0;i<params.length;i++)
    {
        parm = params[i].split('=');

        if(parm[0].toLowerCase() == 'consumer')
        {
            consumer = parm[1];
            break;
        }
    }

    if (consumer.toLowerCase() != 'nol')
    {
        pos = newsSymbol.indexOf(':');

        if(pos < 0)
            qtSymbol = newsSymbol;
        else
            qtSymbol = newsSymbol.substr(pos+1);

        top.document.location = 'http://www.nasdaq.com/symbol/' + qtSymbol.toLowerCase();
    }
    else
        return;
}

      <p> (RTTNews.com) - The Canadian stock market is solidly in negative territory at the start of the new trading week, with nearly every sector trading in the red. Mining, energy and gold stocks are among the weakest performers, due to weakness in commodity prices. Crude <a href="http://www.nasdaq.com/markets/crude-oil.aspx">oil prices</a> have dropped to around $38 per barrel, its lowest level in over 5 years.</p>
      <p>Markets in Europe are holding onto modest gains, but are well off their early highs. Germany's industrial production rebounded in October after two months of decline. European Central Bank president Mario Draghi also said the easy policy is having its "intended" effects, and the bank sees "no particular limit" to how it can use various tools.</p>
      <p>Markets in the United States are pulling back in early trade Monday. A lack of major U.S. economic data is keeping some traders on the sidelines ahead of the Federal Reserve's highly anticipated monetary policy meeting next week. The Fed is widely expected to announce an increase in interest rates following the meeting.</p><div id="articleText-makead"><script type="text/javascript">makeAd('4','300x250','mktsnews','article','','');</script></div>
      <p>On Friday, the index closed up 34.10 points or 0.26 percent, at 13,358.77. The index scaled an intraday high of 13,374.16 and a low of 13,268.43.</p>
      <p>The Diversified Metal and Mining Index is declining 6.49 percent. HudBay Minerals (HBM.TO) is sinking 11.58 percent and Teck Resources (TCK-A.TO) is down 1.34 percent. First Quantum Minerals (FM.TO) is falling 6.45 percent and Lundin Mining (LUN.TO) is losing 5.28 percent. Capstone Mining (CS.TO) is decreasing 6.94 percent and Sherritt International (S.TO) is lower by 2.08 percent.</p>
      <p>The Energy Index is decreasing 5.42 percent. Crude oil prices fell Monday morning amid demand concerns and the imminent US interest rate hike.</p>
      <p>Encana (ECA.TO) is weakening by 6.94 percent and Crescent Point Energy (CPG.TO) is lower by 5.87 percent.  Cenovus Energy (CVE.TO) is declining 4.53 percent and Imperial Oil (IMO.TO) is falling 3.34 percent. Canadian Natural Resources (CNQ.TO) is dropping 5.64 percent and Enbridge (ENB.TO) is surrendering 3.97 percent. Canadian Oil Sands (COS.TO) is decreasing 4.48 percent and Suncor Energy (SU.TO) is down 3.41 percent. Pacific Exploration &amp; Production (PRE.TO) is sinking 9.64 percent.</p>
      <p>The Gold Index is falling 1.94 percent. Gold prices have turned lower this morning, after early action had prices nearly flat.</p>
      <p>Goldcorp (G.TO) is decreasing 1.76 percent and Yamana Gold (YRI.TO) is losing 2.53 percent. Barrick Gold (ABX.TO) is declining 2.41 percent and B2Gold (BTO.TO) is lower by 1.80 percent. Eldorado Gold (ELD.TO) is falling 0.69 percent and Royal Gold (RGL.TO) is surrendering 1.74 percent. Kinross Gold (K.TO) is down 1.75 percent and IAMGOLD (IMG.TO) is weakening 2.69 percent.</p>
      <p>The Capped Materials Index is also down 2.12 percent. Agnico Eagle Mines (AEM.TO) is falling 0.59 percent and Franco-Nevada (FNV.TO) is declining 1.19 percent. Silver Wheaton (SLW.TO) is decreasing 1.93 percent and Agrium (AGU.TO) is lower by 1.19 percent. Potash Corp. of Saskatchewan (POT.TO) is also losing 1.82 percent.</p>
      <p>The Capped Industrials Index is lower by 1.52 percent. Finning International (FTT.TO) is falling 0.83 percent and AutoCanada (ACQ.TO) is declining 4.14 percent. Canadian Pacific Railway (CP.TO) is losing 1.92 percent and Canadian National Railway (CNR.TO) is surrendering 2.04 percent. Bombardier (BBD-A.TO) is also weakening by 2.14 percent.</p>
      <p>The heavyweight Financial Index is falling 1.38 percent. Royal Bank of Canada (RY.TO) is losing 1.21 percent and Toronto-Dominion Bank (TD.TO) is down 0.84 percent. National Bank of Canada (NA.TO) is declining 0.65 percent and Bank of Montreal (BMO.TO) is lower by 1.82 percent. Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce (CM.TO) is dropping 1.87 percent.</p>
      <p>Bank of Nova Scotia (BNS.TO) announced the launch of its new Scotia Wealth Management brand. The stock is decreasing 2.07 percent.</p>
      <p>The Capped Information Technology Index is down 0.67 percent. Constellation Software (CSU.TO) is falling 0.51 percent and BlackBerry (BB.TO) is losing 0.38 percent. Sierra Wireless (SW.TO) is declining 2.75 percent and Avigilon (AVO.TO) is lower by 5.63 percent.</p>
      <p>The Capped Telecommunication Services Index is falling 0.40 percent. TELUS (T.TO) is decreasing 0.74 percent and Manitoba Telecom Services (MBT.TO) is lower by 0.13 percent.</p>
      <p>The Capped Health Care Index is gaining 0.12 percent. Extendicare (EXE.TO) is rising 0.52 percent.</p>
      <p>Canadian Tire (CTC-A.TO) is losing 1.44 percent, after it recalled the 'Holiday Collection' brand of indoor and outdoor Christmas light strings, supplied by Danson Decor.</p>
      <p>On the economic front, Eurozone investor confidence rose for a second straight month in December to its highest level in four months, mainly due to the European Central Bank's move to add more stimulus to boost the region's economy, results of a survey by Sentix revealed Monday.</p>
      <p>The Sentix investor confidence index climbed to 15.7 from 15.1 in November. Economists had forecast a higher score of 17. The latest reading was the highest since August, when it was 18.4.</p>
      <p>Germany's industrial production rebounded in October after two months of decline, but at a sluggish pace as a slump in the energy output hurt momentum. Industrial production rose a calendar-and-seasonally adjusted 0.2 percent from September, when it declined 1.1 percent, preliminary data from Destatis showed Monday. Economists had forecast an increase of 0.7 percent.</p>
      <p>Permanent job placements in the U.K. increased at the fastest pace in seven months in November, the Report on Jobs compiled by the Recruitment and Employment Confederation and KPMG showed Monday. The number of people placed in permanent jobs rose further in November and the growth was strong and picked up to a seven-month high.</p>
      <p>In commodities, crude oil futures for January delivery are down $1.61 or 4.30 percent at $38.36 a barrel.</p>
      <p>Natural gas for January is down 0.071 or 3.25 percent at $2.115 per million btu.</p>
      <p>Gold futures for February are down $9.30 or 0.86 percent at $1,074.80 an ounce.</p>
      <p>Silver for March is down $0.148 or 1.02 percent at $14.38 an ounce.</p>
