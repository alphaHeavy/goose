            <h1 class="lede-large-content__hed"><span class="lede-large-content__highlight">Airbnb Appoints Head of China Operations After Long Search</span></h1>

            <address class="lede-large-content__byline">Bloomberg News</address>

            <div class="lede-large-content__times">

        <time class="article-timestamp" itemprop="datePublished" datetime="2017-06-01T15:18:34.449Z" data-type="updated" data-status="">

        <div class="body-copy">
            <p>After a long search for an executive to run operations in China, <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="/quote/9865065Z:US" title="Company Overview" rel="nofollow noopener"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="SecurityLink"><meta itemprop="url" content="bbg://securities/9865065Z%20US%20Equity"><meta itemprop="security" content="9865065Z US Equity">Airbnb Inc.</a> named an existing executive, Hong Ge, as vice president in charge of its business in the world&#x2019;s second-largest economy.</p><div class="hardwall" data-position="1"></div><div class="softwall" data-position="1"></div><p>Hong will report to Airbnb Chief Executive Officer Brian Chesky, the vacation-rental company said in an emailed statement Thursday.&#xA0;&#x201C;Hong has unparalleled knowledge of our product and what it needs to be in China,&#xA0;combining a deep technical expertise with an understanding of the local market,&#x201D; Airbnb said.</p><div class="hardwall" data-position="2"></div><div class="softwall" data-position="2"></div><p>Hong&#x2019;s job will be to help Airbnb crack what could be its toughest market to date. The company has spent years raising money and preparing the groundwork for expansion into China -- a country whose local players and laws have humbled tech giants ranging from search giant Google to ride-sharing service <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="/quote/0084207D:US" title="Company Overview" rel="nofollow noopener"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="SecurityLink"><meta itemprop="url" content="bbg://securities/0084207D%20US%20Equity"><meta itemprop="security" content="0084207D US Equity">Uber Technologies Inc.</a></p><div class="hardwall" data-position="3"></div><div class="softwall" data-position="3"></div>

        <div id="outstream-video-1-OQVJQ06K50YN01" class="page-ad" data-position="outstream-video">
            <script class="params" type="application/json">{"contentId":"OQVJQ06K50YN01","position":"outstream","dimensions":{"large_desktop":[[1,8]],"small_desktop":[[1,8]],"tablet":[[1,8]]},"strategy":"always","type":"Outstream Video Native Ad","targeting":{"position":"outstream","url":"/news/articles/2017-06-01/airbnb-hires-hong-ge-to-run-china-operations-after-long-search"},"containerId":"outstream-video-1-OQVJQ06K50YN01"}</script>
            <div class="outstream-video-ad-dummy"></div>
        </div>
        <div id="box-YKg70TV" class="page-ad" data-position="mobile-box">
            <script class="params" type="application/json">{"contentId":"OQVJQ06K50YN01","position":"box","dimensions":{"mobile":[[300,250],[1,1]]},"type":"Mobile Body Box Ad","positionIncrement":1,"targeting":{"position":"box1","positionIncrement":1,"url":"/news/articles/2017-06-01/airbnb-hires-hong-ge-to-run-china-operations-after-long-search"},"containerId":"box-YKg70TV"}</script>
            <div class="mobile-box-ad-dummy"></div>
        </div>
        <p>Late last year, Airbnb began <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-12-06/airbnb-inches-its-way-into-china" title="Airbnb Inches Its Way Into China as It Looks for Local Allies" target="_blank"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="StoryLink"><meta itemprop="suid" content="OHSAGO6TTDS7">storing data</a> relevant to its China operations on local servers to comply with controversial digital security laws that give local authorities access to the information. While the company has offered properties there since 2015, Chesky officially launched its service at a glitzy Shanghai media event as part of a whirlwind tour through Asia.</p><div class="hardwall" data-position="4"></div><div class="softwall" data-position="4"></div><p>A Yale University graduate, Hong has previously worked in software engineering roles at <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="/quote/FB:US" title="Company Overview" rel="nofollow noopener"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="SecurityLink"><meta itemprop="url" content="bbg://securities/FB%20US%20Equity"><meta itemprop="security" content="FB US Equity">Facebook Inc.</a> and Google. His Linkedin profile lists Beijing has his current location. Airbnb has been slow to pick someone to take on the leadership role with Chief Financial Officer Laurence Tosi spending much of his time leading the nascent China subsidiary.</p><p>&#x201C;As with all things related to our China plan,&#x201D; Tosi said in December, &#x201C;we are moving extremely slowly, carefully and &#xAD;deliberately.&#x201D;</p><p>Airbnb&#x2019;s biggest challenge will be home-grown rental platform <a itemscope="itemscope" itemprop="StoryLink" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-04-28/tujia-airbnb-s-rival-in-china-seeking-to-raise-300-million" title="Tujia, Airbnb&#x2019;s Rival in China, Seeking to Raise $300 Million" target="_blank"><meta itemprop="active" content="true"><meta itemprop="type" content="StoryLink"><meta itemprop="suid" content="OP455N6KLVR5">Tujia</a>, which is backed by Ctrip.com International Ltd. -- China&#x2019;s largest online travel agent. The company has been consolidating the local market and boasts 400,000 properties as well as an additional 300,000 via its low-cost sibsidiary Mayi. Tujia is valued at over $1 billion and told Bloomberg in April it was in the midst of raising an extra $300 million to fund an expansion of its operations.</p><p>Another rival, Xiaozhu, is also rapidly expanding and has recently branched into targeting business travelers. The company differentiates itself from rivals by offering services to hosts such as renovation work and cleaners.</p><aside class="inline-newsletter" data-state="ready"></aside>
        <div id="in-article-ClMxdFr" class="page-ad" data-position="in-article">

            <div class="in-article-ad-dummy"></div>

        <p>But all of China&#x2019;s players operate in legal gray area due to the lack of specific regulations to govern the sector. Laws often differ between provinces and can rapidly change, as seen in the ride-hailing space; despite the industry gaining the ability to operate legally from the central government last year, many of its drivers could be forced off the road under laws being enacted in several major cities.</p><p><em>&#x2014; With assistance by David Ramli, and Olivia Zaleski</em></p><ol class="noscript-footnotes"></ol>
