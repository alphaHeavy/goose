		<p>PARMA, Ohio – With Election Day only a few days away, there are a number of issues on the ballots in the cities covered by the <em>Sun Post. </em></p>
<p>Here is a rundown of the issues residents will be voting on Nov. 8.</p>
<p><strong>Parma</strong></p>
<p>Residents in Parma, Seven Hills and Parma Heights will be voting on Issue 118, a school tax levy renewal that is critical to the fiscal future of the Parma City School District. The renewal of the existing levy will provide $8,062,500 to the district.</p>
<p>Parma residents will also be voting on Issues 85, 86 and 87. Issue 85 is a renewal of a tax levy for the police department for maintaining motor vehicles, communications and other equipment. Issues 86 and 87 are for liquor sales at the Sheetz on W. 130<sup>th</sup> Street and Sunday liquor sales at the Moonlite Tavern on Broadview Road.</p>
<p><strong>Brooklyn </strong></p>
<p>Residents of Brooklyn have only one local issue on their ballots. Issue 119 is a levy for general improvements at the Polaris Career Center, formerly Polaris Joint Vocational School.</p>
<p><strong>Independence </strong></p>
<p>Residents of Independence will have two local issues on their ballots. Issue 114 is a renewal of permanent improvement levy for five years (2018-2022). Issue 110 is a renewal of tax levy for the Cuyahoga Valley Career Center.</p>
<p><strong>Seven Hills </strong></p>
<p>Residents of Seven Hills will by voting on the most issues of any municipality in the <em>Sun </em>Post coverage area. Issue 91 is a tax levy renewal of 1.7 mills and an increase of 3.34 mills for payment of permanent and part-time firefighters. Issue 92 is an income tax increase of 0.5 percent.</p>
<p>Also on the Seven Hills ballot are Issues 93 and 94, which concern amendments to the City Charter. Issue 93 would amend the charter to provide that there be no less than five firefighters on each shift. Issue 94 would amend the charter to require a circulator of an initiative, referendum or recall petition to be a registered voter of Seven Hills.</p>
<p>As always, your vote means something, and it is your way of making your community a better place in which to live and work.</p>
