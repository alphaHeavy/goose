Blyth will leave Greenwich by September

By Kaitlyn Krasselt

Published 10:48 am EDT, Friday, June 12, 2015

Blyth (NYSE: BTH), the home fragrance and decor company based in Greenwich, plans to relocate its corporate headquarters to Plymouth, Mass., in an effort to restructure the company and improve profitability.

According to documents filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission April 4, the company plans to move by the end of September when the current office lease on East Weaver Street in Greenwich expires.

The move out of Greenwich is not connected to changes to Connecticut’s corporate tax structure in the recently passed state budget, according to Susan Flinn, spokeswoman for Blyth. Flinn said the move is purely a cost-savings effort as the company-owned space in Plymouth is empty. The company will share the building with PartyLite, a Blyth subsidiary, according to the SEC filings.

The move follows months of poor quarterly reports, the most recent of which showed a 12 percent decline in net sales compared to the same period in 2014.

Robert Goergen Jr., Blyth CEO, said sales were hurt by the increasing strength of the U.S. dollar compared to the euro, as well as a decline in independent sales consultants — a staple of the company’s business structure.

“Absent the adverse currency impact, we experienced year-over-year sales growth in all of our emerging markets as well as in several of our mature markets, including Germany, Austria, the U.K. and Australia,” Goergen said in a statement.

Goergen said the company anticipated a decline in operating profits over 2014, and as a result initiated substantial restructuring efforts to benefit profitability, including the move of the corporate headquarters from Greenwich.

Goergen said the one-time costs of the move will be about $2.1 million, incurred in 2015. According to the SEC filings, the costs of relocation will include employee severance payments, employee benefit payments, retention payments, recruiting fees and outplacement fees, to be recorded in the final three quarters of 2015. The costs could vary based on factors such as the level of employee terminations, according to the documents

Flinn said about 30 employees will be affected by the move.

Blyth headquarters have been in Greenwich since the company’s inception 30 years ago when Robert Goergen Sr. purchased Valley Candle in 1976 in Brooklyn, N.Y.

A year later, Goergen and his colleagues acquired Candle Corporation of America, a Chicago-based company. The company went public under the ticker BTH in 1994.

Today, the business has expanded internationally as a designer and marketer of home decor and fragrances, as well as health, wellness and beauty products, household convenience items, and personalized gifts sold through independent sales consultants.
