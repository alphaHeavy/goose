                        <div class="page-item" data-page-number="1"><div data-paragraph-count="2" class="placement-fixed" id="div-placement-fixed-0.018092914002557525" data-total-word-count="403" data-article-has-video="false" data-article-fixed-placement="articleTop"></div><div id="div-placement-fixed-0.11072556003151657" class="placement-fixed" data-page-count="1" data-page-word-count-total="404" data-total-page-count="1" data-article-fixed-placement="pageTop"></div> <div id="div-placement-para-0.2774508881241984" data-image-at-index="-1" class="placement-para" data-paragraph-word-count-total="48" data-page-paragraph-middle="false" data-page-word-count-so-far="0" data-page-word-count-total="404" data-page-paragraph-count-total="2" data-page-paragraph-count-so-far="1" data-article-word-count-so-far="0" data-article-word-count-total="403" data-article-paragraph-count-so-far="1" data-article-paragraph-count-total="2"></div><p>Investors in Redwood Trust Inc (<a href="/quote/RWT.html">RWT</a>) saw new options become available today, for the August 18th expiration. At <a href="https://www.stockoptionschannel.com/" rel="nofollow">Stock Options Channel</a>, our YieldBoost formula has looked up and down the RWT options chain for the new August 18th contracts and identified the following call contract of particular interest. </p> <p></p>The call contract at the $17.5 strike price has a current bid of 5 cents. If an investor was to purchase shares of RWT stock at the current price level of $17.30/share, and then sell-to-open that call contract as a "covered call," they are committing to sell the stock at $17.5. Considering the call seller will also collect the premium, that would drive a total return (excluding dividends, if any) of 1.45% if the stock gets called away at the August 18th expiration (before broker commissions). Of course, a lot of upside could potentially be left on the table if RWT shares really soar, which is why looking at the trailing twelve month trading history for Redwood Trust Inc, as well as studying the business fundamentals becomes important. Below is a chart showing RWT's trailing twelve month trading history, with the $17.5 strike highlighted in red: <p></p> <img width="450" height="300" src="http://www.tickertech.net/pics/thestreet/21497884183.gif" alt="Loading+chart++2017+TickerTech.com" border="0"> <p></p> Considering the fact that the $17.5 strike represents an approximate 1% premium to the current trading price of the stock (in other words it is out-of-the-money by that percentage), there is also the possibility that the covered call contract would expire worthless, in which case the investor would keep both their shares of stock and the premium collected. The current analytical data (including greeks and implied greeks) suggest the current odds of that happening are 56%. On our website under the <a href="https://www.stockoptionschannel.com/symbol/?symbol=RWT&amp;month=20170818&amp;type=call&amp;contract=17.5" rel="nofollow">contract detail page for this contract</a>, Stock Options Channel will track those odds over time to see how they change and publish a chart of those numbers (the trading history of the option contract will also be charted). Should the covered call contract expire worthless, the premium would represent a 0.29% boost of extra return to the investor, or 1.76% annualized, which we refer to as the <i>YieldBoost</i>. <p></p> <div id="div-placement-para-0.16685654948655992" data-image-at-index="184" class="placement-para" data-paragraph-word-count-total="10" data-page-paragraph-middle="false" data-page-word-count-so-far="48" data-page-word-count-total="404" data-page-paragraph-count-total="2" data-page-paragraph-count-so-far="2" data-article-word-count-so-far="48" data-article-word-count-total="403" data-article-paragraph-count-so-far="2" data-article-paragraph-count-total="2"></div><p style="margin-left: 4px; text-align: center; vertical-align: middle; height: 70px; padding: 5px; width:585px; background-color: #FFFF99; border: 1px dotted #666666; font-family: Trebuchet MS; size: 25px;"><span id="nointelliTXT"><a style="color: #222222; text-decoration: none;" href="https://www.stockoptionschannel.com/slideshows/top-yieldboost-calls-of-mortgage-reits/" rel="nofollow"> <img src="https://www.dividendchannel.com/tslideshow.gif" width="16" height="16" border="0" style="margin:0; display:inline; padding-right: 4px"> <b><u>START SLIDESHOW</u></b>:<br><b>Top YieldBoost Calls of the Mortgage REITs »</b></a><b> </b></span></p> <p></p>The implied volatility in the call contract example above is 23%. Meanwhile, we calculate the actual trailing twelve month volatility (considering the last 251 trading day closing values as well as today's price of $17.30) to be 20%.
